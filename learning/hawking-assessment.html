The honest summary

This is an unusually strong, intellectually honest self-learner who has genuinely understood Hawking 1975 rather than memorized its conclusion. The conceptual command (D1, 8.5) is the real article: the student has collapsed the entire effect into one structural insight — the horizon symmetry is a boost with a fixed point at the bifurcation surface, forcing ξ=κλk, hence λ∝e^{κu}, hence thermality — and states outright that 'the non-affinity, the exponential, and the Hawking temperature are the same fact.' That is the deep reading a Wald or an Unruh would nod at, and it is the student's own synthesis (verified in why-horizon-non-affine.html). The intellectual honesty (D4, 8.5) is the most trustworthy signal in the dossier because it is behavioral, not asserted: catching a real sign error, demanding the dx-measure rotation phase be tracked (a subtlety most postdocs never raise), refusing the Gamma integral as a black box, and — tellingly — refusing to fake the 'in your own words' sections of the per-paper note rather than overclaim. The complex-analysis engine at the heart of the result (branch point where the log argument vanishes, imaginary power rotating to a REAL exponential, opposite Wick rotations for α and β, ε→0⁺ pinning the rotation direction) is held cold. Now the blunt part. The headline claim — 'reproduced the full ⟨N⟩ derivation by hand, nothing skipped' — is directly contradicted by the student's own ledger, which I confirmed on disk: the Hawking1975 note is marked 'skimmed', the TL;DR / key-idea / derivation-spine fields are blank placeholders, and the checkbox 'Reproduce the Bogoliubov derivation on a single sheet of paper, no notes' is UNCHECKED. By the student's own stated bar ('if you can't write this without re-opening the paper, you don't understand it yet'), the closed-book test is self-declared unmet. What exists is a beautiful, decompressed, open-book exposition — strong circumstantial evidence of command, but NOT the same skill as a cold whiteboard reconstruction, which is strictly harder. Two load-bearing steps are asserted even within that exposition (both verified): κ=1/4M is reached by calling the direct computation 'degenerate' at f=0 and switching formulas without doing the limit, and the per-mode spectrum leans on 'mode normalization absorbed', gliding past the δ(0)/wave-packet regularization that is the honest way to make ⟨N⟩ finite per mode. On research transfer (D6, 5.5) the orientation is excellent — a concretely scoped first-paper lane (greybody factors for an under-studied spacetime via Page 1976), critical awareness of the islands/Page-curve literature, correct identification of the fixed-background no-backreaction assumption as THE limit — but not one generalization has been executed: no Euclidean derivation of T, no standalone Unruh calculation, no greybody factor computed in any limit, Kerr/RN/fermions quoted not derived. First-paper readiness specifically requires producing a new correct calculation unaided, and there is as yet zero evidence of an independent technical result. The disposition is that of a real researcher; the missing piece is demonstrated independent production, not understanding.
