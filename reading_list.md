## Foundations — General Relativity & QFT in curved spacetime

- [ ] Wald, *General Relativity* (textbook) — ch. 6, 8, 12, 14
- [ ] Birrell & Davies, *Quantum Fields in Curved Space* — ch. 1–4, 8
- [ ] Carroll, *Spacetime and Geometry* — ch. 5, 6 (Schwarzschild, more general BHs)
- [ ] Parker & Toms, *Quantum Field Theory in Curved Spacetime* — for modern treatment

## Hawking radiation — original & classic

- [ ] Hawking, "Particle creation by black holes," *Commun. Math. Phys.* **43**, 199 (1975)
- [ ] Bardeen, Carter, Hawking, "The four laws of black hole mechanics" (1973)
- [ ] Unruh, "Notes on black-hole evaporation," *Phys. Rev. D* **14**, 870 (1976)
- [ ] Page, "Particle emission rates from a black hole," *Phys. Rev. D* **13**, 198 (1976)
- [ ] Gibbons & Hawking, "Cosmological event horizons, thermodynamics, and particle creation" (1977)

## Information paradox — classic & modern

- [ ] Page, "Average entropy of a subsystem" (1993)
- [ ] Page, "Information in black hole radiation" (1993) — the Page curve
- [ ] Almheiri, Marolf, Polchinski, Sully (AMPS), "Black holes: complementarity or firewalls?" (2012)
- [ ] Maldacena & Susskind, "Cool horizons for entangled black holes" (ER=EPR, 2013)
- [ ] Penington, "Entanglement wedge reconstruction and the information paradox" (2019)
- [ ] Almheiri, Engelhardt, Marolf, Maxfield, "The entropy of bulk quantum fields and the entanglement wedge of an evaporating black hole" (2019)
- [ ] Penington, Shenker, Stanford, Yang, "Replica wormholes and the black hole interior" (2019)
- [ ] Almheiri et al., "The entropy of Hawking radiation" — review (2020)

## Numerical work

- [ ] Page, "Particle emission rates from a black hole. II/III" — greybody factors for Schwarzschild
- [ ] Frolov & Novikov, *Black Hole Physics* — numerical methods chapters
- [ ] Modern: BlackHoleSpectra / spectroscopy literature (Berti, Cardoso reviews)

- [ ] Steinhauer, "Observation of quantum Hawking radiation... in an analogue black hole" (2016)
- [ ] de Nova et al., "Observation of thermal Hawking radiation... in an analogue black hole" (2019)
