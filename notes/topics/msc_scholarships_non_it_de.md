# MSc Physics — non-Italy / non-Germany shortlist (2026-05-27)

Web-research agent output, verified against program homepages. Filter:
US / Canada / UK / Netherlands / Sweden / Denmark / Switzerland / France
(English-medium) / Austria / Belgium / Norway. Italy and Germany
deliberately excluded. PSI (Canada) and Cambridge Part III (UK) already
covered separately — see [`msc_scholarships.md`](msc_scholarships.md) and
[`psi_application_strategy.md`](psi_application_strategy.md).

## The hard caveat that surfaced during verification

Sameer's software-engineering BSc collides with **explicit prerequisite
walls** at almost every top theoretical-physics MSc:

- Stockholm: "at least 90 higher education credits in physics" — fail.
- Copenhagen / NBI: "120 ECTS in physics-equivalent topics" — fail.
- Imperial QFFF: "first class in physics or mathematics" — fail without
  the physics undergrad.
- Edinburgh Mathematical Physics: "degrees in engineering… unlikely to
  be suitable preparation" — fail by explicit exclusion.
- ETH theoretical physics: physics-BSc-equivalent required — fail.

The four programs below are the *realistic intersection* of two
constraints: (a) the program admits a non-physics-BSc applicant
without an automatic prerequisite reject, and (b) some real Nepal-
eligible full-funding channel exists.

## The single primary recommendation outside PSI

**Leiden, MSc Physics (Theoretical Physics track).**

The only program on this list whose published admission text *explicitly*
admits "any other discipline" provided physics prerequisites can be
demonstrated. Sameer's engineering BSc is not an automatic reject. Leiden's
theoretical group (Instituut-Lorentz lineage; Schalm/Schoutens on AdS/CFT
and emergent gravity) is a near-perfect topical match for the path-2A
interests in [`../../learning/index.html`](../../learning/index.html).

**Honest tradeoff:** funding is the weak point. LION is partial. To make
Leiden work he must either (a) self-fund the gap (~€15k/yr), unrealistic
on Nepali income, (b) win an external Nepal-eligible award (Holland
Scholarship is €5k partial; Orange Knowledge Programme is closed), or
(c) document the physics prerequisites through a bridging year of MITx /
edX advanced-physics certificates *before* applying, which strengthens
candidacy for LION+departmental top-ups.

Choose Leiden if you want the cleanest admissibility story and are
willing to chase external funding hard. Choose Stockholm only if you
commit to a year of formal physics coursework first to clear 90 ECTS.

## The US — direct answer

**No.** A non-physics BSc Nepali applicant landing in a US theoretical-
physics program with full funding for autumn 2027 is not realistic.

- Top US physics departments (MIT, Princeton, Caltech, Harvard, Stanford)
  admit directly to the PhD, not to a terminal MSc.
- PhD admission effectively requires a physics undergrad and Physics GRE
  scores in advanced coursework Sameer hasn't formally taken.
- Terminal MSc programs that exist in the US (NYU, BU, Columbia MA) are
  almost universally self-funded cash cows. They will not fund a Nepali
  applicant to study theoretical physics.
- The Fulbright Foreign Student Program (Nepal) is the only theoretical
  full-funding lever, but it places students into US grad programs that
  have separately admitted them — it does not solve the admissions wall,
  only the money.

**Strategic recommendation: treat the US as a PhD destination for ~2029,
after a European/Canadian MSc has converted the file from "engineer with
physics interest" to "physicist with publications." Do not spend 2026–
2027 application energy on US MSc applications.**

## What this means for the broader strategy

Excluding Italy and Germany costs real options. ICTP (Italy) is *built*
for Sameer's profile; DAAD/Germany is the only multi-uni full-funding
safety net with the prerequisite-flexibility Sameer needs. Without them,
the realistic full-funding non-physics-BSc paths reduce to:

1. **PSI (Canada)** — primary, [`psi_application_strategy.md`](psi_application_strategy.md)
2. **Stockholm + SISGP (Sweden)** — only if a 90-ECTS physics bridging
   year is done first.
3. **Leiden (Netherlands)** — strong admissibility, weak funding.
4. **KCL / Oxford OMMS (UK)** — strong programs, partial funding via
   GREAT or Oxford-specific scholarships.

PSI becomes even more central than it already was. It is one of the
very few full-funding-for-non-physics-BSc paths in the entire English-
speaking world.
