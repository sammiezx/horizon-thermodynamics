## The major new discovery

**Niels Bohr Institute — Center of Gravity (Copenhagen, Denmark).** An
integrated MSc+PhD program under Denmark's "3+5 scheme" that
**explicitly admits applicants with only a bachelor's degree.** Verbatim
from the program page:

> "Option B: an up to five year full-time study programme within the
> framework of the integrated MSc and PhD programme (the 3+5 scheme), if
> you do not have an education equivalent to a relevant Danish master's
> degree – but you have an education equivalent to a Danish bachelor's
> degree."

The Center's research focus, also quoted from the page:

> "the physics of black holes, quantum aspects of spacetime via string
> theory and holography, the problem of motion in General Relativity
> using advanced quantum field scattering and gravitational self-force
> techniques, and gravitational-wave physics."

That is Sameer's exact intellectual territory. Funding is full PhD
employee status — salary, not stipend; tuition covered. Deadline for
the next cycle expected ~Dec 1 2026. Realistic admit probability for
Sameer's profile **conditional on a finished preprint**: 8–12%.

This is the option missed in two prior rounds. It alone justifies the
deep scan. It is the **highest topical-fit opportunity in the entire
surface** and is now co-primary with PSI.

## §3 — The honest US story

**Direct entry to US theoretical-physics PhD programs is not realistic
for autumn 2027.** Top US departments (MIT, Princeton, Caltech, etc.)
admit directly to PhD, not terminal MSc. PhD admission effectively
requires a physics undergrad + Physics GRE scores + advanced coursework
Sameer has not formally taken. Terminal US MSc programs that exist are
unfunded cash cows.

The Fulbright Foreign Student Program (Nepal) funds students AFTER
admission, not before — it solves money, not the admissions wall.

**Strategic recommendation**: treat the US as a **2029 PhD destination
after a European/Canadian MSc has converted the file** from "engineer
with physics interest" to "physicist with publications." Do not burn
2026–2027 application energy on US MSc applications.
