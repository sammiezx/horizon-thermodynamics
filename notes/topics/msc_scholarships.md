### Why these four for Sameer specifically

- **ICTP** is essentially built for this exact profile: a Nepali engineer with strong technical receipts who needs a credentialed physics-bridge year. Nepal is an eligible developing country; the diploma's stated mission is feeding such candidates into top PhDs. **The single most aligned program in the world for this applicant.**
- **PSI** is the highest-prestige theoretical-physics master's that *explicitly* opens the door to non-physics undergrads. His greybody-factors preprint + AI engineering receipts give a uniquely legible story. Full funding, 1-year, strong feeder pipeline into top PhDs in QG/holography. Long-shot but high payoff.
- **DAAD/Germany** is the safety net with depth. Multi-university strategy reduces single-point-of-failure risk. Strong theory groups (LMU TMP, Bonn, Heidelberg, Göttingen — all with QFT/string/holography faculty).
- **Cambridge Part III + Gates** is the moonshot lottery ticket. Apply, but don't plan on it.

### Programs **dropped after verification**

- **Erasmus Mundus QuanTEEM** — current 2025-2027 cohort is engineering-quantum-tech, not the theoretical/holography track. Next call uncertain.
- **Imperial QFFF** — no automatic full funding for non-UK; would need Commonwealth (Nepal eligibility unstable for new round).
- **ETH theoretical physics** — ESOP highly competitive; master's normally requires closely-equivalent BSc; non-physics is a hard wall.
- **École Polytechnique / ENS Lyon** — Eiffel doesn't cover full tuition at the deepest theory tracks; admission heavily biased to physics backgrounds.

## §5 — Honest risk assessment

The biggest risk is **not** the missing physics transcript — it's the **letter-writer profile**. Admissions committees can rationalise away a non-physics BE if (a) there is a preprint they can read AND (b) there is a *physicist* whose name they recognise saying "I worked with this person and he reasons like a physicist." Without (b), the preprint reads as a clever engineer's hobby project and the committee defaults to rejection because there is no calibration anchor. Current letter pool (Trobus colleagues, NCIT faculty) cannot solve this — none of them can credibly evaluate physics ability.

**Realistic mitigation:** between now and September 2026, convert one cold-email into a real collaboration with one physicist (postdoc-level is fine; doesn't have to be a famous PI). Offer them numerical infrastructure in exchange for being a co-author or acknowledged contributor on a paper, plus a letter. The greybody-factors numerics are *exactly* the kind of work an early-career theorist would happily collaborate on, because the engineering bottleneck is real. **This is the single highest-leverage action between now and application season — higher leverage than any course, exam, or essay revision.**

The secondary risk is that ICTP, by far the best-fit program, runs on an unpredictable annual call cycle and the 2027-28 dates are not yet posted. Subscribe to the ICTP diploma mailing list this week and treat the application as a hard deliverable the moment the call opens.
