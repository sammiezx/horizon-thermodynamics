## Why PSI is a serious target for *this* applicant

**Topical alignment.** Perimeter's nine declared research areas include
Quantum Gravity, Quantum Fields and Strings, Quantum Information, Strong
Gravity, Mathematical Physics, and Cosmology. That set covers essentially
every direction Sameer cares about:

- Hawking radiation, greybody factors, information paradox → Strong Gravity + Quantum Gravity
- Emergent gravity, Jacobson 1995, AdS/CFT → Quantum Fields and Strings + Quantum Gravity
- HaPPY codes, holographic quantum error correction, replica wormholes → Quantum Information + Quantum Gravity
- Numerical / computational physics on the side → Mathematical Physics

No other master's program in the world concentrates this combination as
tightly as Perimeter does. PSI students get year-long exposure to faculty
working on *exactly* the path-2A topics that are Sameer's eventual research
destination.

**Format.** One year. Fully funded — tuition + stipend + accommodation + meals.
Cohort of ~30. Designed as a launch pad into PhDs at Perimeter, Caltech,
Princeton, the IAS, MIT, Cambridge DAMTP, etc.

**Eligibility.** Non-physics undergraduates *explicitly admissible.*

> "Candidates with the required background knowledge of physics but without a
> degree in physics are welcome to apply to PSI." — PSI FAQ

That single sentence makes PSI the strongest theoretical-physics master's in
the world that Sameer can credibly apply to.

## The application aperture — what they actually look at

Without a physics transcript, the file rests on:

1. **The 300-word self-study summary** (PSI FAQ): "a short (300 words) summary
   of how you acquired the prerequisite background knowledge. You may wish to
   include books read, online courses taken, events attended, etc."

   This is the single highest-leverage document in the entire application.
   It is where the Hawking 1975 close-reading, the Jacobson 1995/2003 study,
   the greybody-factors preprint, the self-driven coursework, and any
   collaboration with a physicist all converge.

2. **Demonstrable competency in five prerequisite areas:**
   - Classical mechanics
   - Electromagnetism
   - Special relativity
   - Quantum mechanics
   - Statistical physics

3. **Standard application materials** (transcripts where available, statement
   of purpose, CV, 2–3 recommendation letters, English proficiency for
   non-native speakers).

4. **Implicit signals**: arXiv preprint, public GitHub of numerical work,
   physicist letter writers, evidence of having engaged with primary
   literature (the Hawking 1975 / Jacobson 2003 annotated readings in
   `learning/index.html` are *exactly* this kind of artifact).

PSI explicitly notes they will "contact shortlisted candidates who are
missing any application materials" — so the shortlisting decision is what
matters. After that, gaps get worked out.

## Five-month application-prep calendar (June 2026 → October 2026)

This is the *PSI-specific* slice of the broader scholarship calendar in
[`msc_scholarships.md`](msc_scholarships.md). Same anchors, different focus.

### June 2026 — gap analysis + topic lock-in

- Pick the specific spacetime for the centerpiece preprint (the open
  decision in [`../../manuscripts/greybody-factors/README.md`](../../manuscripts/greybody-factors/README.md)). PSI readers will
  ask about your physics reasoning, not just your code — finalising the
  spacetime is what lets the rest of the year be productive.
- Self-audit the five prerequisite areas. For each, rate your current
  understanding from "could pass an undergrad final" down to "haven't
  seriously touched this." Honest assessment; the gaps determine July.
- Subscribe to the PSI application alert list at
  [perimeterinstitute.ca/training/masters-program/psi-how-apply](https://perimeterinstitute.ca/training/masters-program/psi-how-apply).

### July 2026 — close the prerequisite gaps with *artifacts*

For each of the five areas, complete a real problem-set sequence that
produces a public artifact (GitHub repo with solved problems, blog posts,
or worked PDFs). Suggested anchors:

- **Classical mechanics** → Taylor *Classical Mechanics* problems, or
  David Tong's Cambridge Part II Classical Dynamics notes problem sets.
  Probably your strongest area from engineering school.
- **Electromagnetism** → Griffiths *Introduction to Electrodynamics*
  chapters 5–12, with worked problems. This is usually the weakest area
  for engineers transitioning into physics.
- **Special relativity** → already absorbed through Hawking 1975 §1–2 and
  the annotated reading at [`../../learning/index.html`](../../learning/index.html). Tighten with
  Schutz *A First Course in General Relativity* §1–3.
- **Quantum mechanics** → Griffiths *Introduction to Quantum Mechanics*,
  or Shankar *Principles of Quantum Mechanics* (harder, more aligned with
  PSI culture). At minimum, work through to time-dependent perturbation
  theory.
- **Statistical physics** → Tong's Cambridge Statistical Physics notes
  with problem sets. Probably weakest area; budget the most time here.

By end of July, the public GitHub should show 5 directories, each with a
solid sequence of solved problems. PSI cannot grant you a physics
transcript, but they can see that you have functionally produced one.

### August 2026 — physicist letter-writer collaboration

This is the single highest-leverage action in the entire plan (see
risk assessment in [`msc_scholarships.md`](msc_scholarships.md) §5).

Cold-email 10 postdoc-level physicists by mid-August:

- ICTS Bangalore numerical-relativity postdocs
- SISSA / ICTP greybody-factor authors
- Perimeter / CITA postdocs (especially anyone working on Strong Gravity)
- Tribhuvan / Kathmandu University physics faculty (Nepal-based connection)
- Any author of a paper you've genuinely read and engaged with

Pitch (one paragraph + a link to the workspace): *here is the numerical
greybody-factor work I'm doing, here is the public code, here is what's
working, here is where I'd value your input. I'm not asking for credit —
I'm asking whether a half-hour of your time could improve this work, and
whether there might be a small collaboration we could write together.*

Expect 2 responses out of 10. Work with 1. By late August, you want one
physicist who has read drafts, given feedback, and can write a letter
that says specifically "Sameer reasons like a physicist, not like an
engineer copying physics formulas."

### September 2026 — SOP + cold-read

Draft the PSI Statement of Purpose. Structure:

1. The path (BE Software → Trobus data engineering → growing-quietly-into-physics)
2. The artifacts (greybody preprint, public coursework, this workspace)
3. The fit (which Perimeter faculty's work resonates and why — name 3–4)
4. The trajectory (PSI → PhD in QG / holography / quantum info)

Have it cold-read by:

- Your collaborating physicist (technical fit)
- One non-physics academic (does the story land?)
- One PSI alumnus, if you can find one via LinkedIn or Twitter (does it
  fit the genre?)

### October 2026 — submit within the first 2 weeks of opening

Applications open in October 2026. Submit early, not late.
Late submission with the same materials is strictly worse than early
submission — early files get more eyeballs, more discussion time, and
shortlisting often happens in waves.

## Honest risk-adjusted prognosis

PSI admits ~30 students from ~700+ applicants annually. The applicant
pool is dominated by strong physics-undergrad candidates from top
international institutions. A non-physics-BSc applicant is the
exception, not the norm.

That said: every year PSI admits a handful of non-traditional candidates,
*specifically* because the FAQ explicitly invites them. The realistic
admit probability for an applicant in Sameer's category — non-physics
undergrad + engineering career + serious self-study + an arXiv preprint +
a physicist letter — is plausibly in the 5–15% range. Without the
preprint and the physicist letter, it is well below 5%. With both, well
within "worth applying."

The portfolio strategy in [`msc_scholarships.md`](msc_scholarships.md) handles the
remaining uncertainty: PSI is the reach, ICTP is the anchor, DAAD is
the safety. Apply to all three.
