## §1 — The headline find

**NYU Abu Dhabi Global PhD Fellowship in Physics.** This is the single
best new addition this round.

- **Why it matters**: Ahmed Almheiri is on the NYUAD Physics faculty.
  He is one of the authors of the islands formula (AEMM 2019) and a
  central figure in modern black-hole-information-paradox work — your
  path-2A territory.
- **Structure**: 4-year fully-funded PhD. Year 1 coursework at NYU New
  York (backfills any physics-undergrad gap); years 2-4 research at
  NYUAD.
- **Funding**: tuition, fees, housing in Abu Dhabi + rental contribution
  in NYC, health insurance, travel support, stipend.
- **Admissibility**: NYUAD says (for sister PhD programs) "degrees do
  not have to be in a particular discipline" provided mastery is
  demonstrated. *Verify by email for the Physics program specifically.*
- **Deadline**: Dec 1–15, 2026 for Fall 2027 intake. Applications open
  mid-Sept 2026.
- **Admit probability**: ~5–8%. Highly selective ("students from more
  than 120 countries") but Sameer's profile + the Almheiri topical
  match is unusually clean for a non-physics-BSc applicant.
- **URL**: nyuad.nyu.edu/en/academics/graduate-and-phd/phd-programs/global-phd-student-fellowship-in-physics.html

This is the only program found this round that simultaneously (a) opens
explicitly to non-physics BSc, (b) fully funds internationals, and (c)
houses a flagship Sameer-topic PI.

## §2 — The other Tier-A additions

### LSU Physics & Astronomy PhD (Louisiana, US)

- Site says "U.S.-equivalent bachelor's" — does NOT gate on physics
  specifically.
- **Nepal is explicitly listed among source countries of current
  students.** Direct cultural signal.
- "Essentially all students accepted into the Department are offered
  financial support" — tuition waiver + assistantship salary + most
  health insurance.
- Hosts LIGO Livingston ties; gravity theory + experimental.
- Deadline: standard US Dec 15, 2026 / Jan 1, 2027.
- Admit probability: ~10–15% conditional on a clean preprint.
- **Action item**: email the LSU graduate coordinator with CV +
  preprint draft before Oct 2026 — Nepal-source-country signal makes
  this a high-leverage cold contact.

### Penn State Physics PhD / Institute for Gravitation and the Cosmos (IGC)

- Bulletin: "A bachelor's degree in physics **or an allied field** is
  required." "Allied field" is interpretable for engineering with a
  strong preprint — but it's a real but narrow door.
- IGC = Ashtekar / Eisenstein / Laguna lineage; one of the most
  concentrated loop-quantum-gravity / numerical-relativity hubs in the
  US.
- Tuition + stipend full duration via fellowships/TA/RA.
- Deadline: January 15, 2027.
- Admit probability: ~5–10%.

### ICTS-TIFR Bangalore Integrated PhD

- **Engineering BE explicitly admissible**: "Bachelor's degree in
  Science, **Engineering** or Medicine ≥3 years."
- Strong gravity/string group (continuing Spenta Wadia / Shiraz Minwalla
  lineage on string theory + holography).
- Entrance exam (TIFR GS) ~Dec 2026 for 2027 entry.
- Stipend ₹37,000–42,000/mo.
- Admit probability: ~8–12%.
- **South Asia geography helps**; cost-of-application is shared with
  TIFR Mumbai (same entrance exam).

### TIFR Mumbai Integrated PhD

- Verified bachelor-eligible (BSc/BE/BTech ≥3 years).
- Overseas applicants explicitly invited via Subject Board of Physics
  with CV + 3 referrals.
- Same entrance exam infrastructure as ICTS Bangalore.
- Admit probability: ~10% (Indian academic-prestige opacity is the
  main uncertainty).

## §3 — Tier-B additions (lower priority but worth knowing)

### University of Canterbury (New Zealand) — David Wiltshire's gravity group

- "Strong background in statistics, physics, mathematics or a related
  discipline" — explicit non-physics-friendly language.
- NZ$35,000/yr tax-free stipend + tuition fees, 3 years.
- Rolling deadline; flexible start dates.
- Lower prestige than ANU but genuinely open. ~15–25% admit.

### HKPFS (Hong Kong PhD Fellowship Scheme) routed via CUHK / HKUST / HKU

- HK$340,800/yr stipend + travel for 3 yrs. Total ~US$230K.
- HKUST physics: "Students with a first degree in an area other than
  Physics may be required to take additional courses." Open in
  principle.
- **HKPFS initial application: 1 Dec 2026** for 2027 entry — same week
  as NBI deadline.
- Very competitive (400 fellowships, all fields, global pool). CUHK GW
  group is the warmest target. ~3–7% admit.

### Sydney + USYDIS / Monash OzGrav node / ANU CGA

- Australia generally: supervisor-driven HDR model; admit-prob depends
  heavily on whether a supervisor pre-commits.
- **Monash OzGrav (Thrane/Mandel/Lasky)** is genuinely undervalued for
  someone with data-engineering strengths — they explicitly welcome
  "physics, computer science, mathematics or closely related" at the
  postdoc tier, which signals the PhD culture.
- ANU CGA (Susan Scott) is astrophysics-heavier, partial match to
  QI×holography interests.

## §5 — On the "PI directly hires a funded student" lens

This **does not work** for US/UK/EU top theory PIs (Harlow, Hartman,
Headrick, Penington, Maxfield, Schalm). At all of these institutions,
PhDs are admitted by the department first, then choose a PI in year
1–2. A PI cannot directly hire a bachelor's-only international PhD
student around the department admissions committee.

**The PI-direct-hire model only exists at:**

1. **NYU Abu Dhabi Global PhD** — Almheiri reachable via a structured
   non-physics-friendly route. (Tier A above.)
2. **Perimeter Institute** — only via PSI MSc → partner-uni PhD. PSI
   is already the primary target.
3. **Some EPFL / EU labs** — supervisor-pre-commit + national
   fellowship layered; speculative.

**Realistic mechanism for Harlow / Hartman / Headrick et al.**: PSI
MSc → strong recommendation → standard US/Canadian PhD admission.
That's the path you've already mapped. **No PI-direct shortcut.**
